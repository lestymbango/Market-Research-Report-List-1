<p><p>Electrostatic precipitators (ESPs) are air pollution control devices that use electrical charges to remove particulates from exhaust gases. The market for ESPs is primarily categorized into wet and dry types. Wet ESPs utilize water to enhance particle capture efficiency, making them suitable for sticky or fine particulates. Dry ESPs, on the other hand, operate without water, offering lower maintenance and energy costs. Other market types may include baghouse collectors or hybrid systems, combining various technologies for improved performance and efficiency.</p></p>
