<p><p>In the dental consumables market, restorative materials are categorized into direct and indirect types. Direct restorative materials, like amalgam and resin ionomers, are applied immediately during dental procedures. Indirect materials, such as ceramics, gold alloys, and porcelain fused to metal, are fabricated outside the mouth and then bonded to the tooth. Each material offers unique properties; ceramics provide aesthetic solutions, metals offer durability, while glass ionomers and resin ionomers facilitate adhesion and fluoride release, enhancing overall oral health.</p></p>
