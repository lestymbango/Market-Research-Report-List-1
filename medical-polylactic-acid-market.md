<p><ul><li>L-Polylactic (PLLA)</li><li>D-Polylactic (PDLA)</li><li>DL-Polylactic (PDLLA)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The medical polylactic acid (PLA) market consists of three main types: L-Polylactic Acid (PLLA), D-Polylactic Acid (PDLA), and DL-Polylactic Acid (PDLLA). PLLA is known for its biocompatibility and slower degradation, making it ideal for long-term implants. PDLA has a more rapid degradation rate, suitable for transient applications. PDLLA, a copolymer of L and D forms, offers a balance of properties, allowing customization for various medical uses such as sutures, scaffolds, and drug delivery systems.</p></p>
