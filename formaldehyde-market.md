<p><p>The formaldehyde market can be segmented into several types based on production methods, with silver-catalytic oxidation and iron-molybdenum cofactor being notable examples. Silver-catalytic oxidation involves using silver as a catalyst to convert methanol to formaldehyde, offering high efficiency and purity. In contrast, the iron-molybdenum cofactor method utilizes a complex of iron and molybdenum to facilitate the reaction, providing an alternative pathway with distinct cost and operational benefits. Both methods cater to varying industrial applications and market demands.</p></p>
